Site Visit Strategy & Plan

A Systematic 10-Step Methodology

Methodology Overview

To ensure a comprehensive and systematic evaluation of The Stadthuys' competitiveness, our team developed a structured 10-step approach. This methodology enabled us to gather quantitative data and qualitative insights through careful planning, organized execution, and thorough post-visit analysis.

Our strategy emphasizes professional organization, clear task distribution, collaborative data collection, and evidence-based assessment to produce reliable findings that can inform tourism management decisions.

10-Step Site Visit Plan

01

Online Research

Search for information about Stadthuys Museum online. Check entrance fee, opening hours and location. Learn about the history and educational value of the museum. Ensure the site is suitable for learning and assignment purposes.

02

Visit Planning

Decide the date, day and time of the visit. Estimate the duration of the visit. Prepare a simple budget (entrance fee, food, transportation).

03

Transportation & Cost Sharing

Rent a car to make movement easier and save time. Share the car rental and fuel cost equally among group members.

04

Movement Schedule & Time Management

Prepare a tentative schedule to organize activities efficiently. Set specific times for: departure and arrival; museum exploration; photo and video sessions; interviews with tourists or staff. Follow the schedule to avoid wasting time. Prioritize important activities if there is a delay. Be flexible and adjust timing if needed.

05

Group Task Distribution

Assign roles before the visit: one person takes photos and videos; one person interviews tourists or staff; one person takes notes and records information; one person oversees and monitors group progress. This helps make work organized and efficient.

06

Visitor Observation Strategy

Observe visitor behavior inside the museum. Notice which areas attract more visitors. Observe how visitors interact with exhibits and information boards. This helps understand visitor experience and tourism value.

07

Communication & Interview Strategy

Use polite and simple questions during interviews. Keep questions short and respectful. Ask about visitor satisfaction, museum attractions and suggestions. Avoid disturbing other visitors or staff.

08

Documentation Strategy

Take clear photos and short videos as evidence. Write notes during the visit. Save all materials properly.

09

Professional Behaviour & Ethics

Dress neatly and behave professionally. Follow museum rules and regulations. Speak politely and respect other visitors. Protect the museum property.

10

Post-Visit Discussion

Hold a group discussion after the visit. Share what each member learned. Discuss challenges and new knowledge gained. Relate the experience to tourism and hospitality studies.

Task Distribution & Responsibilities

Photographer

Responsibilities: Capture high-quality images of building exterior, interior spaces, exhibits, facilities, visitor flow, signage, and potential issues (vandalism, structural concerns). Document visual evidence supporting competitiveness assessment across all indicators.

Interviewer

Responsibilities: Conduct brief, respectful visitor surveys to gather insights on satisfaction levels, expectations vs. reality, recommendations, and overall experience. Record audio of informal conversations and ambient museum sounds for multimedia portfolio.

Note-Taker

Responsibilities: Systematically document observations for each competitiveness indicator, record scores in assessment forms, note specific examples and evidence, maintain timeline of observations, and ensure all data is captured for later analysis and reporting.
